Frederique Prince, cpa.

This background allows Frederique to approach financial challenges holistically, drawing connections across disciplines to create innovative, sustainable solutions for organizations.

With a commitment to building financially sound and efficient organizations, Frederique has consistently driven impactful financial strategies. Her work spans from overseeing corporate finances to implementing cost-effective processes, ensuring that every dollar spent aligns with the organization’s mission. Her past roles have required balancing efficiency with precision, enabling organizations to thrive under fiscally responsible leadership.

As a Board Member of Yukon Energy, Frederique plays a vital role in overseeing the organization responsible for providing a stable, affordable, and sustainable energy supply to the Yukon. Her experience in corporate governance ensures that decisions are both accountable and aligned with long-term sustainability goals, directly impacting the wellbeing and future of the territory’s residents. Frederique’s governance expertise brings a balance of oversight and innovation to a complex, high-stakes sector.

In her role as Finance Director at Parks Canada Yukon, Frederique is at the financial helm of initiatives that protect some of Canada’s most remarkable natural landscapes, including Kluane National Park—the largest non-polar icefield in the world—and Vuntut National Park, home to a vast caribou herd. Her work ensures that conservation efforts are financially sustainable, balancing budgets and resource allocations to preserve these national treasures for future generations.

Across her diverse career, Frederique has demonstrated strong leadership skills, guiding teams and driving organizational success. Her roles have included managing cross-functional teams and implementing strategic financial plans, with a particular focus on empowering teams to deliver excellence. Frederique’s ability to foster collaboration and lead change initiatives has been key to her success in high-impact environments.
